# PMS and Period Pain Management

## What is PMS?

Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS) includes physical and emotional symptoms that occur 1-2 weeks before your period.

## Common PMS Symptoms

### Physical
- Bloating
- Breast tenderness
- Headaches
- Fatigue
- Food cravings
- Acne

### Emotional
- Mood swings
- Irritability
- Anxiety
- Depression
- Difficulty concentrating

## Natural Pain Relief Methods

### 1. Heat Therapy
- Hot water bottle on lower abdomen
- Warm bath with Epsom salts
- Heating pads

### 2. Movement
- Gentle yoga (child's pose, cat-cow)
- Walking
- Swimming
- Avoid high-intensity if pain is severe

### 3. Nutrition
- Anti-inflammatory foods: ginger, turmeric
- Magnesium: dark chocolate, bananas, nuts
- Omega-3: fish, flaxseeds, walnuts
- Avoid: caffeine, alcohol, very salty foods

### 4. Herbal Remedies
- Chamomile tea for relaxation
- Ginger tea for nausea and cramps
- Peppermint tea for bloating

### 5. Acupressure
- Press the point 3 finger-widths below navel
- Massage lower back in circular motions

## Lifestyle Changes

1. Regular exercise throughout the month
2. Stress management (meditation, deep breathing)
3. Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
4. Reduce caffeine and processed foods
5. Stay hydrated

## When Pain Relief Isn't Enough

If home remedies don't help or pain is severe:
- Keep a symptom diary
- Consult a gynecologist
- May indicate endometriosis or PCOS
- Professional treatment options available
