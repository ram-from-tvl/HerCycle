# Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

## What is a Menstrual Cycle?

The menstrual cycle is a monthly process where the body prepares for pregnancy. It typically lasts 21-35 days, with an average of 28 days.

## Phases of the Cycle

### 1. Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)
- Period bleeding occurs
- Hormone levels are low
- Energy may be lower
- Focus on rest and gentle movement

### 2. Follicular Phase (Days 1-14)
- After period ends
- Estrogen rises
- Energy increases
- Good time for challenging workouts

### 3. Ovulation (Day 14)
- Egg is released
- Peak energy and mood
- Highest fertility window

### 4. Luteal Phase (Days 15-28)
- After ovulation
- Progesterone rises
- PMS symptoms may appear
- Energy gradually decreases

## Common Symptoms

- Cramps and lower back pain
- Mood changes
- Breast tenderness
- Bloating
- Fatigue
- Headaches

## Self-Care Tips

1. **Nutrition**: Eat iron-rich foods, stay hydrated
2. **Movement**: Gentle exercise can reduce cramps
3. **Rest**: Listen to your body, sleep well
4. **Heat**: Heating pad for cramp relief
5. **Tracking**: Keep a cycle journal

## When to See a Doctor

- Periods lasting longer than 7 days
- Very heavy bleeding
- Severe pain that interferes with daily life
- Irregular cycles (varying by more than 7 days)
- No period for 3 months (if not pregnant)
